The Sixteenth Flash gathers several short letters in which Nursî answers
questions his students had raised, and almost every answer turns into a lesson in
how to think.

Why, his friends ask, do men of sainthood sometimes foretell things that never
happen? Because, Nursî replies, foreseen events can be **conditional**: as a
Hadith says that almsgiving can turn aside a calamity, so a promised relief may
hinge on a condition — here, the community's sincere prayer — and lapse when the
condition goes unmet. This is his picture of **destiny** (*kader*) as often
suspended, not blindly fixed. Asked why he does not strike back at the officials
who harass him, he answers with one of his governing principles: the wound of the
age is belief corroded by misguided science, and only **light** can heal a heart
— "the club" of politics would merely drive unbelief inward into hypocrisy. For
the same reason he opposes war (it would tear his young students from their work)
and counsels showing the Risale carefully.

A second letter meets atheist objections to the Qur'an by way of its language.
The sun "setting in a muddy spring" (18:86) records how the distant Atlantic
*looked* to a traveller, not an astronomical claim; the Barrier of Dhū'l-Qarnayn
and Gog and Magog are a universal image with many real instances, such as the
Great Wall. A third explains the **"five hidden things"**: the timing of rain and
the nature of the unborn stay with God because rain and life are pure mercy
(*rahmet*), deliberately left unveiled by fixed law so that thanks and prayer are
never made idle — instruments read only their approaching signs, not the Unseen
itself.

The Flash closes on an idea central to all of Nursî's thought. Look at a mirror
*for the glass* and you miss the face it reflects; look *through* it and you see
the face. So every creature can be read two ways: "for itself," which is where
materialism loses its way in heedlessness (*gaflet*), or as a **letter**
(*mânâ-yı harfî*) pointing beyond itself to its Maker. The original develops each
answer with more colour and care; the full text is linked above.
