The Seventeenth Flash is not a single treatise but a string of **fifteen short
Notes**, distilled from Arabic jottings Nursî had written years before as
reminders to himself. Many are addressed inward — "O heedless Said!" — which
gives the Flash its intimate, bracing tone: a man arguing his own soul out of
its illusions.

The early Notes work on the heart's attachments. Do not, the first urges, tie
your love to what death will strip away; the second sets down a Qur'anic
balance — worship nothing above yourself, and let nothing make you proud
(*enaniyet*, the ego, undone from both sides); the third names the spell of
**heedlessness** (*gaflet*), by which the passing world is mistaken for solid
ground. The fourth turns the seasons into an argument: a Maker who each spring
restores life in familiar form is announcing the **resurrection** to come. Other
Notes look outward and are sharply of their time: the fifth treats imported
materialist civilisation as a "sickness of the heart"; the sixth steadies a
believer shaken by the sheer numbers of unbelievers, insisting that worth lies in
quality, not quantity; the seventh warns the zealous moderniser not to break the
bonds that hold a people to its faith; the eighth finds a quiet mercy in labour —
God has hidden the wage for work inside the work itself.

The later Notes rise toward God directly. The ninth places **prophethood**
(*nübüvvet*) and **belief** (*îmân*) at the foundation of every human good; the
tenth counsels that the knowledge of God (*marifetullah*) is seen only by a
whole, generous gaze, not a fault-finding one; the eleventh admires the Qur'an's
patient, accessible manner with ordinary people; the thirteenth offers a freeing
rule — attend to your own duty and leave to God what is God's to manage. The
fourteenth and fifteenth close on signs of divine oneness, among them the image
of a palace built of jewels gathered from across the earth, which can only point
to a single Maker.

Read whole, the Notes are less an argument than a series of awakenings; in
Nursî's own words each carries a force a summary can only gesture at. The full
text is linked above.
