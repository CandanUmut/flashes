The Thirty-Third Flash is a short notice. It gathers the **first works of the
"New Said,"** written in Arabic and "inspired in his heart by reality" —
*Katre*, *Habbe*, *Şemme*, *Zerre*, *Hubab*, *Zühre* and *Şûle*, with their
appendices. Their Turkish translations have been collected in *Mesnevi-i Nûriye*,
another part of the Risale-i Nur, rather than reproduced here.

This companion entry records only where those works live; there is no separate
treatise to summarise at this place. (The Seventeenth Flash's "Fifteen Notes" are
themselves drawn from several of these same early treatises.)
