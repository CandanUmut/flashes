The Fifth Flash is not a treatise but a short note. Nursî explains that he had
meant to write a work unfolding, in fifteen "degrees," a deep sense of the verse
"God suffices us, and He is the best Disposer of affairs" (3:173) — but set it
aside, because it belonged more to reflective remembrance than to reasoned
exposition. And the important treatise that had first been called the Fifth Flash
— *The Stairway of the Practices of the Prophet and Antidote for the Sickness of
Innovation* — turned out to comprise eleven points, and so was designated the
**Eleventh Flash**. The Fifth Flash's place was therefore left unoccupied.

This companion entry simply records that fact, so the numbering reads clearly;
there is no separate treatise to summarise. For the work that fills its intended
place, see the Eleventh Flash; the page is linked above.
