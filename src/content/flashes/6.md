Like the Fifth, the Sixth Flash is a brief note rather than a developed
treatise. Nursî had intended a work that would trace, through fifteen or twenty
"degrees" of reflection and remembrance, the truth carried in the phrase **"There
is no power or strength save through God, the Exalted, the Mighty"** (*lâ hawla
wa lâ quwwata illâ billâh*) — a confession that all strength and capacity belong
to God alone. These, he says, were degrees he had perceived within himself on
his own inner journey.

Because the piece leaned more toward illumination and devotional taste than
toward reasoned knowledge, he thought it better placed at the end of the Flashes
than set among them — and so, like the Fifth, its sequential place was left as a
note. This companion entry records that, with no separate treatise to summarise;
the page is linked above.
